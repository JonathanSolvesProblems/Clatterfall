# Demo script: Clatterfall (target 75 seconds)

The focus test: this whole video is about **one** feature, the daily marble run. If a beat doesn't serve "the whole sub built this machine and it runs every morning," cut it.

Record on a phone-shaped viewport (portrait). Use a machine with real placed parts from the pilot cohort, not the bare seed.

---

**[0:00–0:08] COLD OPEN on the money shot.**
Black, then a marble drops into frame. It threads a sprawling wood-and-brass contraption, camera following, sparks and woodblock clacks on every impact.
> VO: "Every morning, one marble runs the machine that a whole subreddit built together."

**[0:08–0:18] The stakes.**
The marble reaches the current end and the brass ruler shows yesterday's record just ahead. Caption: "Yesterday it stalled here." Camera snaps to a few glowing empty cells.
> VO: "You get one part a day. You add it right here, where the marble runs out."

**[0:18–0:30] Place a part.**
Tap a glowing frontier cell. The palette slides up. Pick a curved chute, it snaps into the cell with a ratchet clunk. Another player's part pops in beside it, live.
> VO: "One player, one part, once a day. That's all you get. Nobody builds this alone."

**[0:30–0:42] The scale.**
Drag up the board to reveal the whole machine winding down the screen, part after part after part.
> VO: "Everybody builds it. And it only grows."

**[0:42–0:58] Tomorrow's run, the payoff.**
Cut to the next morning's run. The marble now threads through the new parts and travels further. As it nears the record, playback slows, the camera leans in, and the ruler's vermilion record-pin slides forward. Confetti. Result card: "NEW RECORD, 2,183 px deep. Your part carried it +269 px."
> VO: "Tomorrow the marble runs again, and everyone comes back to see how far your part carried it."

**[0:58–1:12] Close.**
Quick hits: the streak counter, the goal basin glowing far below, a comment thread arguing what to build next.
> VO: "Clatterfall. It's r/place, but it's a machine, and it runs every single day."

**[1:12–1:15]** Logo card on the sunlit board; the marble drops in one last time.

---

### Notes
- Lead with the run, not the rules. The 8-second descent is the whole pitch.
- Keep it under 90 seconds. The value prop is simple, so the demo should be too.
- The demo post itself must be self-explanatory: the splash card states the pitch, and returning players auto-play the run on open.

### Personal open (use in the Devpost writeup; optional 4s cold-open on the video)
Lead the written submission with the true origin story, then the number, then the run:
> "I collected marbles as a kid and kept a box of them. Clatterfall is one marble the whole subreddit shares: **__ redditors, one machine, __ px deep over __ days.**"

Keep the video itself opening on the marble run (the strongest visual). If you want the story in the video, use a single 4-second title card before the money shot, not a talking-head intro.
